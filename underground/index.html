A proposed fund focused on 3 green infrastructure initiatives:

1. Tunnels for Power Lines to Restore Tree Canopy

California is posed to underground power lines to reduce forest files. Petra, a robotics company that focuses solely on underground utilities, is using new boring technology that swiftly cuts through the hardest rocks using hot gas to avoid the need to replace cutting blades.

During the early 21st century, Grant Street was de-forested to clear the airspace for power lines that traverse the neighborhood. When the current poles are ready to be replaced in 50 to 100 years, our fund will assist in covering the extra cost for tunnels.

2. Green Hydrogen Pipeline - Waste Biomass to Jet Fuel

In Western Australia, the “Hazer process” (Hydrogen And Zero Emissions Research) uses iron oxide as a catalyst to turn methane (CH4) in wastewater into hydrogen, while separating the carbon to produce graphite - a saleable by-product used in building materials, lithium-ion batteries, water treatment, lubricants and many other applications.

Western Australia - carbon-negative hydrogen project
Hazer's commercial pilot in Western Australia will convert biogas into hydrogen and graphite, a solid form of carbon. More details

A facility could be located near the Atlanta airport to convert Grant Parks waste water into jet fuel and green hydrogen.

3. Underground Highspeed Transportation

Funding for construction of routes that move vehicles quickly underground to create safer pedestrian and bike areas above.

When near-surface cut-and-cover trenches are used, this fund is limited to narrow trenches (less that 35' wide) to allow for tree canopy and pedestrian friendly crossings.
